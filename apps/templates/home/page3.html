The High Court is the supreme judicial body in a state. According to Article 214, each state of India shall have a High Court. However, Article 231 also mentions that there can be a common High Court for two or more States or for two or more states and a union territory.

Civil courts: Civil courts deal with civil cases. Civil law is applied in disputes when one person sues another person or entity. Examples of civil cases include divorce, eviction, consumer problems, debt or bankruptcy, etc.

Criminal Courts: Criminal Courts are established to impart justice to people and punish the offenders that go against the law. Criminal courts are established to solve serious crimes such as assault, robbery, murder, arson, rape and other kinds of crimes.
